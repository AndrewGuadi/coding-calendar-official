replacingOccurrences(of:with:options:range:)

The `replacingOccurrences(of:with:options:range:)` method in Swift is used to return a new string in which all occurrences of a specified substring are replaced with another substring. This can be useful for tasks like correcting misspellings, changing specific words or phrases in a text, or formatting content. You can specify options for the replacement process, such as case sensitivity, and you can limit the scope of the replacement to a specific range within the string.

text = 'The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.'

newText = text.replacingOccurrences(of: 'fox', with: 'cat')

# Result: newText = 'The quick brown cat jumps over the lazy dog.'
text = 'All apples are not green.'

newText = text.replacingOccurrences(of: 'apples', with: 'fruits', options: .caseInsensitive)

# Result: newText = 'All fruits are not green.'
text = 'She likes ice cream. Ice cream is her favorite.'

newText = text.replacingOccurrences(of: 'ice cream', with: 'sorbet', options: [], range: text.range(of: 'ice cream')!)

# Result: newText = 'She likes sorbet. Ice cream is her favorite.'
